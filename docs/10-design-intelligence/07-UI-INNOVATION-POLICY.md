# UI Innovation Policy

## 1. Policy Statement

Innovation is encouraged. Consistency is mandatory. NexoraXS designs products to solve business problems—not to create beautiful screenshots detached from real work.

AI and human designers should think like product designers: understand context, reduce risk, improve outcomes, measure behavior, and preserve maintainability. UI/UX Pro Max is used to expand the candidate space; it does not approve novelty.

## 2. Innovation Principles

1. **Start with evidence.** Identify the user, problem, current behavior, and consequence before generating a concept.
2. **Improve an outcome.** Innovation must target completion, clarity, accuracy, speed, decision quality, accessibility, confidence, or recovery.
3. **Preserve architecture.** A novel interface cannot relocate ownership or hide a cross-OS dependency.
4. **Reuse the grammar.** New concepts use shared tokens, primitives, language, and state semantics unless an approved proposal demonstrates why they cannot.
5. **Prefer the smallest effective change.** Do not replace a workflow when a bounded improvement solves the problem.
6. **Make intelligence legible.** New AI experiences expose provenance, explanation, limits, choice, and action impact.
7. **Design for maintenance.** A concept is incomplete without responsive, bilingual, accessible, testable, documented behavior.
8. **Measure after launch.** Visual enthusiasm is not evidence of business value.

## 3. Product Design, Not Dribbble Design

NexoraXS rejects presentation-first concepts characterized by:

- decorative dashboards with no decision purpose;
- glass, glow, gradients, 3D, or animation used as a substitute for hierarchy;
- hidden labels or unfamiliar icons chosen for minimal appearance;
- impossible data, perfect states, or fabricated business impact;
- interactions that work only with a mouse or large screen;
- English-only composition later mirrored for Arabic;
- dense novelty that ignores training, support, or operational pressure;
- AI decoration without explanation or accountable action boundaries.

Visual craft matters, but it serves comprehension, trust, and efficiency.

## 4. Innovation Levels

| Level | Example | Governance |
|---|---|---|
| L0 Correction | Contrast, focus, typo, missing label, broken direction | May proceed within an approved task and component contract |
| L1 Bounded improvement | Clearer state, error recovery, spacing, responsive adaptation | Requires design review and quality checklist; no learned behavior change |
| L2 Pattern evolution | New variant, shared pattern, changed interaction semantics | Requires design proposal, accessibility review, migration and specification |
| L3 Experience redesign | New layout, navigation, dashboard, or workflow | Requires three-option proposal and explicit approval before implementation |
| L4 Architecture-affecting concept | Changes owner, context model, domain boundary, or cross-OS dependency | Stop; requires architecture governance before design approval |

## 5. Evidence Ladder

Use the strongest available evidence:

1. observed task failure, accessibility defect, support evidence, or validated user research;
2. product analytics with correct scope and interpretation;
3. repeated usability findings or operational feedback;
4. audit evidence and code/current-state inspection;
5. established platform pattern and relevant design research;
6. UI/UX Pro Max candidate guidance;
7. informed hypothesis clearly labeled for validation.

Lower-level evidence may justify exploration, not confident claims.

## 6. Innovation Brief

Before concept generation, document:

- user, role, and context;
- business problem and current evidence;
- architectural and UI owner;
- constraint and non-goal;
- expected outcome and success measure;
- existing components/patterns to preserve;
- bilingual, accessibility, device, privacy, and performance needs;
- legacy compatibility and migration risk.

## 7. Exploration Rules

- Generate meaningfully different options rather than cosmetic variations.
- Include the current design or minimal change when it may be the best answer.
- Prototype the highest-risk assumption first.
- Test content and state extremes, not only the ideal path.
- Use realistic but non-sensitive representative data.
- Validate Arabic/RTL and English/LTR during exploration, not after selection.
- Include keyboard, focus, screen-reader, reduced-motion, dark, and responsive behavior.
- Identify components and tokens that would be reused or affected.

## 8. Consistency Budget

A deliberate divergence is acceptable only when:

- the existing pattern materially fails the documented task;
- reuse or a bounded variant cannot resolve the failure;
- the new behavior is learnable and accessible;
- the proposal states where divergence begins and ends;
- migration, documentation, testing, and rollback are defined;
- the responsible owner explicitly approves it.

Divergence in visual styling alone is not innovation.

## 9. Experimentation

An experiment must specify:

- hypothesis;
- eligible users and protected groups;
- primary and guardrail measures;
- privacy and consent constraints;
- accessibility-equivalent variants;
- duration or stopping rule;
- no-harm and rollback conditions;
- owner and approval.

Do not experiment on security, tenant isolation, canonical ownership, required consent, or critical accessibility. Do not use deceptive patterns to improve a metric.

## 10. AI Innovation Rules

AI may broaden exploration and synthesize evidence. It must:

- follow `04-AI-DESIGN-RULES.md`;
- identify Skill-derived candidates;
- avoid inventing research or metrics;
- preserve deterministic and canonical boundaries;
- submit redesign through the mandatory Proposal A/B/C format;
- wait for explicit approval before altering an approved experience.

## 11. Success and Failure

Innovation succeeds when it produces a demonstrable improvement without increasing architectural ambiguity, accessibility barriers, support burden, or long-term inconsistency.

Stop, revise, or roll back when:

- users misunderstand scope, owner, or consequence;
- completion, error, abandonment, or recovery worsens;
- Arabic/RTL or assistive-technology use is materially weaker;
- performance disrupts operational work;
- a cross-OS or canonical boundary becomes unclear;
- the change requires undocumented exceptions;
- evidence does not support continued divergence.

## 12. Approval Gate

No innovative concept becomes an approved NexoraXS design until its evidence, alternatives, architecture alignment, component impact, quality checklist, migration plan, and explicit approvals are recorded in the applicable product specification.
