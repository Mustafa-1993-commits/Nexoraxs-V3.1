> **Planning-only boundary:** This matrix states what evidence a future human review could require.
> It does not accept evidence, satisfy a prerequisite, select an authority-change class, authorize
> a batch, or permit an edit. Current controlling authority remains unchanged. Missing evidence is
> a review blocker, not permission to supply an assumption.

`AIB` identifiers are Wave 4 planning references only. `AC` and `SM` identifiers remain Wave 3
review references only. None is a canonical Governance ID, API value, database value, runtime
state, workflow, state machine, implementation requirement, or engineering task.

## 2. Evidence Interpretation Rules

1. A source proposal explains intent but does not override current authority.
2. Current authority is cited as read-only evidence; listing it does not approve it for editing.
3. Conflict evidence must include the unchanged conflict status and both sides of any tension.
4. Open Question evidence may justify resolution, explicit deferral, refinement, or rejection only
   at a separately authorized human gate; Wave 4 leaves every question open.
5. Stakeholder evidence is advisory until an authorized decision records its effect.
6. A prerequisite is not satisfied by a prior AI review, document presence, branch state, or lack
   of objection.
7. Minimum review packages contain evidence and proposed scope only, never final replacement text.
8. If a prohibited assumption is necessary to continue, the future batch stops at that boundary.
